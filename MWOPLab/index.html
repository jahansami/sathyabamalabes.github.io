Experiment

Hours Of lab class

Experiments

Study electromagnetic waves in the microwave range (1 GHz to 300 GHz), microwave circuits, components and measurement techniques. Explore wave polarization, reflection, refraction, and absorption.

Study light and optics, optical components like lenses, mirrors, prisms, filters and measurement techniques. Explore light propagation, reflection, refraction, and diffraction, interferometry, polarization, and laser physics.

Both labs involve designing and building circuits and systems using electromagnetic waves, measuring and analyzing wave behavior and device performance. Work with advanced instrumentation and software tools for experiments and data analysis.

Experiment 1

STUDY OF MICROWAVE COMPONENTS

The main objective of this experiment is to get acquainted with all sorts of microwave components and other passive devices and also get familiarized with the microwave components that are available in our laboratory.

Experiment 2

CHARACTERISTICS OF THE REFLEX KLYSTRON TUBE

To study the repeller mode characteristics of the Reflex Klystron Tube and to determine its different mode.

Experiment 3

V-I CHARACTERISTICS OF GUNN DIODE

Study of V-I characteristics of Gunn diode.

Experiment 4

HARACTERISTICS OF ISOLATOR AND CIRCULATOR

To study the X-band Microwave Isolator and Circulator.

Experiment 5

CHARACTERISTICS OF E - PLANE TEE, H - PLANE TEE AND MAGIC TEE

To study the characteristics of E - plane Tee, H - plane Tee and Magic Tee.

Experiment 6

MEASUREMENT OF RADIATION PATTERN AND GAIN OF HORN ANTENNA

To obtain directional pattern of a Horn Antenna.

Experiment 7

VSWR MEASUREMENT

To determine the Standing-Wave Ratio and Reflection Coefficient.

Experiment 8

CHARACTERISTICS OF DIRECTIONAL COUPLER

To study the function of directional coupler by measuring the Coupling factor.

Experiment 9

INTRODUCTION TO FIBER OPTICS COMMUNICATION

The light followed the curved path of water. The light was guided and a new science was born. This was due to a property of light called refraction.

Experiment 10

SETTING UP A FIBER OPTIC ANALOG LINK

To obtain intensity modulation of the analog signal, transmit it over a fiber optic cableand demodulate the same at the receiver and to get back the original signal.

Experiment 11

SETTING UP A FIBER OPTIC DIGITAL LINK

To obtain intensity modulation of digital signal, transmit it over fiber optic cable anddemodulate the same at the receiver end to get back the original signal.

Experiment 12

MEASUREMENT OF NUMERICAL APERTURE

To measurement of the Numerical Aperture (NA) of Optical Fiber.

Experiment 13

PULSE WIDTH MODULATION AND DE MODULATION IN OPTICAL FIBER

To study Pulse Width Modulation in Optical Communication.

Experiment 14

MEASUREMENT OF PROPAGATION LOSS AND BENDING LOSS IN OPTICAL FIBER

To measure propagation or attenuation loss and bending loss in optical fiber.
